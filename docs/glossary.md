# TSAM Glossary

This glossary provides working definitions for recurring terms used in the TSAM public repository. These definitions are operational and may be revised as validation work progresses.

## TSAM

**Trinity Space Arbitration Model.** A reality-constrained research framework for organizing cosmological and astrophysical anomaly audits across multiple observational layers.

## Reality-constrained reasoning

A methodological rule that gives priority to external observational evidence over internally self-consistent theoretical construction.

## Observational audit

A structured check of whether a claim, residual field, directional pattern, or anomaly remains stable under fixed rules and independent data comparison.

## Fixed-rule validation

A validation process in which the rules are defined before interpreting the result, reducing post-hoc pattern fitting and subjective adjustment.

## Freeze-review cycle

A research-control procedure in which a hypothesis or parameter set is frozen before review, then tested against external constraints before further expansion is allowed.

## Provisional claim

A claim that remains open to revision, downgrade, or rejection because it has not yet passed sufficient independent validation.

## Cross-dataset consistency

The requirement that a proposed structure should not depend only on one dataset or one coordinate representation. Stronger claims require agreement across independent observational layers.

## Residual field

A structured difference between expected and observed behavior after a chosen baseline model or prior has been applied.

## Dynamical prior

An assumed background rule or model used before interpreting motion, residuals, or inferred structure. TSAM-related audit work treats prior choice as a possible source of systematic bias.

## Reference-frame robustness

The degree to which an inferred residual structure remains stable when coordinate choices, reference frames, or dynamical priors are changed.

## CRIF

**Coordinate-Free Kernelized Residual Inference Framework.** A related research framework focused on residual inference under potentially misspecified reference frames or dynamical priors.

## SPARC layer

The audit layer based on galaxy rotation-curve data, used to test residual structures and baryonic mismatch patterns.

## QSO layer

The audit layer based on quasar redshift-space structure and cross-epoch continuity checks.

## LRG layer

The audit layer based on luminous red galaxy and large-scale galaxy-density fields.

## CMB layer

The audit layer based on cosmic microwave background directional or large-scale features.

## Solar-system boundary layer

A low-redshift or local-frame consistency layer used to test whether proposed large-scale interpretations conflict with nearby dynamical constraints.

## Academic archive

A versioned Zenodo DOI record or related public archive entry preserving a research snapshot. Archive existence is not equivalent to independent validation.

## Public claim boundary

A documentation boundary separating what has been archived, what has been tested, what remains hypothetical, and what is only a future research direction.
