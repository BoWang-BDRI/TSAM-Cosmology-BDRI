# TSAM Research Roadmap

## Phase 1 — Public Theory Index

Goal: maintain a clear public-facing explanation of TSAM concepts, archive links, and validation boundaries.

Tasks:

- Maintain the repository README.
- Keep Zenodo archive references visible.
- Separate theoretical claims from validation methods.
- Record updates in the research log.

## Phase 2 — Residual and Reference-Frame Auditing

Goal: develop a clean residual-audit layer that can test whether anomalies remain stable under changes in model assumptions or reference-frame construction.

Key direction:

- Expand CRIF-related explanations.
- Clarify coordinate-free residual inference.
- Define what counts as a robust residual structure.
- Track failure modes and artifact risks.

## Phase 3 — SPARC Rotation Residual Track

Goal: use galaxy rotation datasets as a local dynamical-prior stress test.

Planned audit questions:

- Where do baryonic predictions exceed observed rotation values?
- Are residual structures radially coherent?
- Do residual fields cluster by galaxy class, radius, or velocity regime?
- Can the residual field be summarized without overfitting?

## Phase 4 — QSO and LRG Large-Scale Structure Track

Goal: evaluate whether quasar and luminous red galaxy datasets show stable directional, phase, or density-wall behavior across redshift slices.

Planned audit questions:

- Are apparent structures stable across catalog versions?
- Are phase offsets consistent or survey-artifact driven?
- Do northern and southern sky partitions show reference-frame discontinuities?
- Can any candidate axis survive randomization and survey-window tests?

## Phase 5 — CMB and Cross-Epoch Consistency Track

Goal: compare candidate directional structures across early-universe and late-universe observational layers.

Planned audit questions:

- Do CMB directional signatures align with later matter-distribution candidates?
- Are candidate structures robust under masking and frequency selection?
- Can cross-epoch alignment survive independent processing choices?

## Phase 6 — Local-System Boundary Track

Goal: test whether local dynamical or boundary-layer effects provide a low-redshift termination layer for TSAM-related hypotheses.

Planned audit questions:

- Are solar-system scale anomalies compatible with large-scale interpretations?
- Can local effects be separated from coordinate or selection artifacts?
- What observations would falsify the local-boundary component?

## Phase 7 — Public Reproducibility Layer

Goal: convert selected analyses into reproducible notebooks, scripts, and audit ledgers.

Planned outputs:

- Dataset notes.
- Fixed-rule audit scripts.
- Validation reports.
- Failure-case documentation.
- Changelogs for theory and method revisions.

## Working Principle

Each phase must preserve the same rule:

> Observational constraint first; theoretical interpretation second.

The roadmap is provisional and should be revised when stronger data, better analysis, or failed validation requires it.
